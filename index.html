Software Engineer — R&D
2023 — 2024

Embedded in a hardware testing environment at Tektronix's R&D division, where the status quo was slow, manual, and expensive. The team was spending significant engineering hours on repetitive PCIe PLL bandwidth tests — precise work that didn't need human hands on it. I automated the entire test workflow using Python and Selenium, integrating AWG-based signal generation directly with oscilloscope control for deterministic, repeatable results every run.

The bigger structural shift was replacing the BERT hardware setup — a costly piece of test infrastructure — with a fully software-driven solution that matched its reliability at a fraction of the cost. This wasn't just about saving money; it made the test suite portable, easier to maintain, and straightforward to extend. It's the kind of change that demonstrates what software thinking, applied rigorously to a hardware-first problem, can actually unlock.
